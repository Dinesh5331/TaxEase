Old Tax Regime vs New Tax Regime - Complete Comparison Guide
India Income Tax - FY 2024-25 and FY 2025-26 (Income Tax Act 2025)

SECTION 1: OVERVIEW OF BOTH REGIMES
The Indian Income Tax system offers two tax regimes for individual taxpayers:
1. Old Tax Regime - Higher tax rates but allows exemptions and deductions
2. New Tax Regime - Lower tax rates but limited deductions (default regime from FY 2023-24)

SECTION 2: TAX SLABS COMPARISON

Old Tax Regime Tax Slabs (FY 2024-25, AY 2025-26):
- Up to Rs. 2,50,000: NIL
- Rs. 2,50,001 to Rs. 5,00,000: 5%
- Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 10,00,000: 30%

For senior citizens (age 60 to 79) under Old Regime:
- Up to Rs. 3,00,000: NIL
- Rs. 3,00,001 to Rs. 5,00,000: 5%
- Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 10,00,000: 30%

For super senior citizens (age 80 and above) under Old Regime:
- Up to Rs. 5,00,000: NIL
- Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 10,00,000: 30%

New Tax Regime Tax Slabs (FY 2024-25, AY 2025-26) under Section 115BAC:
- Up to Rs. 3,00,000: NIL
- Rs. 3,00,001 to Rs. 7,00,000: 5%
- Rs. 7,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000: 10%
- Rs. 10,00,001 to Rs. 12,00,000: 15%
- Rs. 12,00,001 to Rs. 15,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 15,00,000: 30%

New Tax Regime Tax Slabs (FY 2025-26, AY 2026-27) under Income Tax Act 2025 / Budget 2025:
- Up to Rs. 4,00,000: NIL
- Rs. 4,00,001 to Rs. 8,00,000: 5%
- Rs. 8,00,001 to Rs. 12,00,000: 10%
- Rs. 12,00,001 to Rs. 16,00,000: 15%
- Rs. 16,00,001 to Rs. 20,00,000: 20%
- Rs. 20,00,001 to Rs. 24,00,000: 25%
- Above Rs. 24,00,000: 30%

SECTION 3: REBATE UNDER SECTION 87A

Old Tax Regime:
- Rebate of Rs. 12,500 available if total income does not exceed Rs. 5,00,000
- Effective zero tax for income up to Rs. 5,00,000 in old regime

New Tax Regime (FY 2024-25):
- Rebate of Rs. 25,000 if total income does not exceed Rs. 7,00,000
- Effective zero tax for income up to Rs. 7,00,000 in new regime

New Tax Regime (FY 2025-26, Income Tax Act 2025):
- Rebate increased to Rs. 60,000 if total income does not exceed Rs. 12,00,000
- Effective zero tax for income up to Rs. 12,00,000 (twelve lakh rupees) in new regime
- This is the biggest benefit of the new regime under Budget 2025

SECTION 4: STANDARD DEDUCTION
Old Tax Regime:
- Standard deduction of Rs. 50,000 for salaried employees and pensioners

New Tax Regime (FY 2024-25):
- Standard deduction of Rs. 75,000 for salaried employees (increased from Budget 2024)
- Standard deduction of Rs. 25,000 for family pensioners

New Tax Regime (FY 2025-26, Income Tax Act 2025):
- Standard deduction of Rs. 75,000 for salaried employees (continued)
- Family pension standard deduction: Rs. 25,000

SECTION 5: EXEMPTIONS AND DEDUCTIONS ALLOWED

Under Old Tax Regime (exemptions and deductions ALLOWED):
- House Rent Allowance (HRA) exemption under Section 10(13A)
- Leave Travel Allowance (LTA) exemption
- Standard Deduction of Rs. 50,000
- Children Education Allowance up to Rs. 100 per month per child (max 2 children)
- Deduction under Section 80C up to Rs. 1,50,000 (PPF, ELSS, LIC, EPF, NSC, home loan principal)
- Deduction under Section 80D up to Rs. 25,000 for health insurance (Rs. 50,000 for senior citizens)
- Deduction under Section 80E for education loan interest
- Deduction under Section 80G for donations
- Deduction under Section 80CCD(1B) additional Rs. 50,000 for NPS
- Home loan interest deduction under Section 24(b) up to Rs. 2,00,000 for self-occupied property
- HRA, LTA, Transport allowance and other special allowances

Under New Tax Regime (limited deductions ALLOWED):
- Standard Deduction of Rs. 75,000 for salaried employees
- Employer contribution to NPS under Section 80CCD(2) - up to 10% of salary (14% for government employees)
- Agniveer Corpus Fund deduction under Section 80CCH
- Section 80JJAA for employment of new employees
- No HRA exemption allowed
- No LTA exemption allowed
- No Section 80C deductions (no PPF, LIC, ELSS deductions)
- No Section 80D medical insurance deduction
- No home loan interest deduction under Section 24(b)
- No Section 80G donation deductions

SECTION 6: SURCHARGE RATES

Both regimes:
- 10% surcharge: if income exceeds Rs. 50,00,000 but not Rs. 1,00,00,000
- 15% surcharge: if income exceeds Rs. 1,00,00,000 but not Rs. 2,00,00,000

Old Tax Regime only:
- 25% surcharge: if income exceeds Rs. 2,00,00,000 but not Rs. 5,00,00,000
- 37% surcharge: if income exceeds Rs. 5,00,00,000

New Tax Regime:
- Surcharge capped at 25% even for income above Rs. 5 crore (important benefit)

SECTION 7: HEALTH AND EDUCATION CESS
- 4% Health and Education Cess on income tax plus surcharge applies in both regimes

SECTION 8: DEFAULT REGIME
- From FY 2023-24 onwards, New Tax Regime is the DEFAULT regime for all taxpayers
- Taxpayers who wish to use the Old Tax Regime must explicitly opt in
- Salaried employees: inform employer at start of year via declaration
- Non-salaried individuals and businesses: file Form 10-IEA to opt for old regime
- Salaried employees can switch between regimes every year
- Business/profession taxpayers can opt out of new regime only ONCE in their lifetime

SECTION 9: WHEN TO CHOOSE OLD REGIME
Choose Old Tax Regime if:
- You have significant tax deductions like home loan interest, HRA, 80C investments
- Total annual deductions exceed Rs. 3,75,000 (breakeven point approximately)
- You live in a rented house and claim HRA exemption
- You have a home loan and claim interest deduction under Section 24(b)
- You have high 80C investments (max Rs. 1,50,000) - PPF, ELSS, LIC
- You pay medical insurance and claim 80D
- You have children and claim education allowances
- You contribute to NPS and claim 80CCD(1B) extra Rs 50,000

SECTION 10: WHEN TO CHOOSE NEW REGIME
Choose New Tax Regime if:
- Your annual income is below Rs. 7,00,000 (FY 2024-25) or below Rs. 12,00,000 (FY 2025-26) - effective zero tax
- You have minimal deductions (less than Rs. 3,75,000 total)
- You want simplified tax filing without tracking investments
- You are a high income earner above Rs. 5 crore - surcharge capped at 25%
- You are a new employee with no existing tax-saving investments
- You are a senior citizen with no home loan or HRA

SECTION 11: INCOME TAX ACT 2025 - KEY CHANGES
The Income Tax Act 2025 (New Income Tax Bill 2025), effective from FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27):
1. Revised new tax regime slabs - 7 slabs now up to 25% and 30%
2. Rebate increased dramatically: Rs. 60,000 rebate for income up to Rs. 12,00,000 in new regime - effectively zero income tax
3. Nil tax bracket expanded to Rs. 4,00,000 in new regime
4. Standard deduction maintained at Rs. 75,000 for salaried
5. New tax regime remains the default regime
6. Old regime remains available with explicit opt-in
7. TDS threshold on interest income increased to Rs. 50,000 (from Rs. 40,000) for senior citizens
8. TDS on rent threshold increased to Rs. 50,000 per month (from Rs. 50,000 per year)
9. Updated and simplified tax code language - all sections renumbered
10. Crypto/Virtual Digital Assets taxed at 30% flat rate without deductions (continued)
11. Simplified compliance and filing procedures

SECTION 12: BREAKEVEN ANALYSIS
Old vs New Regime breakeven for salaried individual (FY 2025-26):
- If total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + Home loan interest etc.) exceed Rs. 4,25,000, old regime may save more tax
- Generally: New regime better for income below Rs. 12 lakh due to zero tax
- For income Rs. 12-15 lakh with high deductions: old regime may be beneficial
- For income above Rs. 15 lakh with maximum deductions: compare both scenarios
