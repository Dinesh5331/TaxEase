ITR ELIGIBILITY REFERENCE GUIDE - AY 2025-26
============================================
Source: Official CBDT ITR Forms and Income Tax Act 1961/2025
This document lists eligibility criteria, allowed/disallowed income sources,
income limits, and relevant sections for each ITR form.

==============================================================================
ITR-1 (SAHAJ)
==============================================================================

APPLICABLE TO:
- Individual taxpayers ONLY (not HUF, not firms, not companies)
- Resident Indians ONLY (not NRI, not RNOR - Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident)
- Total income must NOT exceed Rs. 50 lakh (Rs. 50,00,000) during the year

ALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- Salary or Pension income (Section 15, Section 17)
- Income from ONE house property only (Section 22, Section 23, Section 24)
- Income from other sources (Section 56): interest from savings/FD, family pension,
  dividends, income from winning lotteries etc.
- Agricultural income up to Rs. 5,000 only

DISALLOWED INCOME SOURCES (must NOT use ITR-1 if you have these):
- Capital Gains income of any kind (Section 45) - short term or long term
- Business income or Profession income (Section 28)
- Income from more than one house property
- Agricultural income exceeding Rs. 5,000
- Foreign income or foreign assets (foreign bank accounts, foreign shares, etc.)
- Income taxable under Section 115BBDA (dividend exceeding Rs. 10 lakh)
- Income under Section 194N (cash withdrawal)
- Deferred tax on ESOP from eligible start-ups

WHO CANNOT USE ITR-1:
- Non-Resident Indians (NRIs)
- Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident (RNOR)
- Individuals who are directors of a company
- Individuals holding unlisted equity shares at any time during the year
- Individuals with income exceeding Rs. 50 lakh
- Individuals with capital gains
- Individuals with business or profession income
- Individuals with more than one house property
- Individuals who have deposited more than Rs. 1 crore in current accounts (7th proviso to Section 139(1))
- Individuals who incurred foreign travel expenses exceeding Rs. 2 lakh (7th proviso to Sec 139(1))
- Individuals whose electricity bills exceed Rs. 1 lakh (7th proviso to Sec 139(1))

SPECIAL CASES:
- Senior citizens (age 60+) CAN use ITR-1 if they meet all conditions above
- Super senior citizens (age 80+) CAN use ITR-1 if they meet all conditions above

TAX REGIME:
- Default regime is New Tax Regime under Section 115BAC(1A)
- Can opt for Old Tax Regime by filing Form 10-IEA under Section 115BAC(6)
  before the due date under Section 139(1)

RELEVANT SECTIONS:
- Section 15 – Salary income
- Section 17 – Definition of salary, perquisites, and profits in lieu of salary
- Section 22 – Income from house property
- Section 23 – Annual value of house property
- Section 24 – Deductions from house property income
- Section 56 – Income from other sources
- Section 139(1) – Mandatory return filing
- Section 115BAC(1A) – Default new tax regime
- Section 115BAC(6) – Opting out of new tax regime

==============================================================================
ITR-2
==============================================================================

APPLICABLE TO:
- Individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUF)
- Resident, Non-Resident (NRI), and RNOR individuals
- NO income limit (total income can exceed Rs. 50 lakh)
- Does NOT have income from business or profession

ALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- Salary or Pension income (Section 15)
- Income from house property (Section 22) - multiple properties allowed
- Capital Gains (Section 45) - both short-term and long-term, including
  - Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) under Section 111A
  - Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) under Section 112 and Section 112A
  - Capital gains on property, shares, mutual funds, bonds, etc.
- Income from other sources (Section 56)
- Foreign income and foreign assets (foreign bank accounts, shares, property)
- Agricultural income (any amount)
- Income from winning lotteries, races, card games (Section 115BB)
- Dividend income (including exceeding Rs. 10 lakh under Section 115BBDA)
- Pass-through income from business trusts or investment funds

DISALLOWED INCOME SOURCES (must NOT use ITR-2 if you have these):
- Income from business or profession (Section 28) - use ITR-3 or ITR-4

WHO CANNOT USE ITR-2:
- Individuals or HUFs with any income from business or profession (Section 28)
- Persons who are partners in firms and have business income from partnership
  (use ITR-3 in that case)

SPECIAL CASES:
- NRIs CAN use ITR-2 (unlike ITR-1 which is only for residents)
- HUFs CAN use ITR-2
- Individuals with multiple house properties MUST use ITR-2 (not ITR-1)
- Directors of companies MUST use ITR-2 (or ITR-3), not ITR-1
- Individuals holding unlisted equity shares MUST use ITR-2 (or ITR-3)

TAX REGIME:
- Can file under Old or New Tax Regime
- Opt out of new regime via Form 10-IEA under Section 115BAC(6)

RELEVANT SECTIONS:
- Section 15 – Salary income
- Section 22 – House property income
- Section 45 – Capital gains
- Section 48 – Mode of computation of capital gains
- Section 54, 54B, 54EC, 54F – Exemptions on capital gains
- Section 56 – Income from other sources
- Section 111A – STCG on equity shares/equity mutual funds
- Section 112 – LTCG on other assets
- Section 112A – LTCG on listed equity shares/equity MFs (above Rs. 1 lakh)
- Section 115BB – Winnings from lotteries
- Section 115BBDA – Dividend exceeding Rs. 10 lakh
- Section 139(1) – Return filing obligation
- Section 115BAC(1A) – New tax regime
- Section 115BAC(6) – Opting out of new regime

==============================================================================
ITR-3
==============================================================================

APPLICABLE TO:
- Individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUF)
- Resident, Non-Resident (NRI), and RNOR individuals
- Having income from Business or Profession (Section 28)
  OR being a partner in a firm (even if no direct business income)
- NO income limit
- NOT eligible for presumptive taxation (Section 44AD/44ADA/44AE) OR has opted
  out of presumptive scheme

ALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- Business or Profession income (Section 28) - mandatory for this form
- Salary or Pension income (Section 15)
- Income from house property (Section 22) - multiple properties allowed
- Capital Gains (Section 45) - short-term and long-term
- Income from other sources (Section 56)
- Foreign income and foreign assets
- Agricultural income
- Partner's share in firm's profit

DISALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- No specific income type is excluded from ITR-3, but if the taxpayer ONLY has
  salary/house property/other sources with no business income, they should use
  ITR-1 or ITR-2, not ITR-3

WHO CANNOT USE ITR-3:
- Individuals and HUFs with NO business or profession income (they should use ITR-1 or ITR-2)
- Companies (use ITR-6)
- Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs (use ITR-5)
- Entities under Section 139(4A) to 139(4F) (use ITR-7)

SPECIAL CASES:
- NRIs CAN use ITR-3 if they have business income
- A partner in a firm MUST use ITR-3 to report partnership income
- Individuals eligible for presumptive taxation who opt out of Section 44AD/44ADA
  must file ITR-3 (not ITR-4)
- Mandatory audit under Section 44AB applies if turnover exceeds threshold

RELEVANT SECTIONS:
- Section 28 – Business or Profession income
- Section 44AA – Maintenance of accounts
- Section 44AB – Tax audit requirement
- Section 44AD – Presumptive taxation for business (if not opted)
- Section 44ADA – Presumptive taxation for professionals (if not opted)
- Section 44AE – Presumptive taxation for transport operators
- Section 45 – Capital gains
- Section 115JC – Alternate Minimum Tax (AMT)
- Section 139(1) – Return filing
- Section 115BAC – Tax regime

ALSO MANDATORY:
- Form 29C (Report under Section 115JC) if AMT exceeds Normal Tax and not opting
  new tax regime

==============================================================================
ITR-4 (SUGAM)
==============================================================================

APPLICABLE TO:
- Resident Individuals ONLY (not NRI, not RNOR)
- HUF (Hindu Undivided Family) - Resident only
- Firms (other than LLP) - Resident only
- Total income must NOT exceed Rs. 50 lakh (Rs. 50,00,000)
- Income from business under PRESUMPTIVE TAXATION scheme only

ALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- Business income under Presumptive Taxation:
  - Section 44AD: Eligible business (turnover up to Rs. 3 crore if 95%+ receipts digital,
    otherwise up to Rs. 2 crore) - 8% or 6% of turnover deemed as profit
  - Section 44AE: Transport operators (up to 10 goods vehicles) - fixed profit per vehicle per month
  - Section 44ADA: Specified Professionals (doctors, lawyers, CAs, architects, etc.)
    with gross receipts up to Rs. 75 lakh (or Rs. 150 lakh if 95%+ receipts digital)
    - 50% of receipts deemed as profit
- Salary or Pension income (Section 15)
- Income from ONE house property (Section 22)
- Income from other sources (Section 56)

DISALLOWED INCOME SOURCES (must NOT use ITR-4 if you have these):
- Capital Gains of any kind (Section 45)
- Foreign income or foreign assets
- More than one house property income
- Agricultural income exceeding Rs. 5,000
- Business income NOT under presumptive taxation scheme

WHO CANNOT USE ITR-4:
- Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) - must use ITR-3 instead
- Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs) - must use ITR-5
- Individuals/HUFs with capital gains
- Individuals/HUFs with income exceeding Rs. 50 lakh
- Individuals who are directors of a company
- Individuals holding unlisted equity shares
- Individuals who have deposited more than Rs. 1 crore in current accounts
- Individuals opting out of presumptive scheme (use ITR-3)
- Individuals claiming DTAA (Double Tax Avoidance Agreement) benefits

SPECIAL CASES:
- Firms (partnership firms, not LLPs) CAN use ITR-4
- If taxpayer was using 44AD and now opts out before 5 years, must use ITR-3
  for next 5 years and cannot use presumptive taxation again

TAX REGIME:
- Default is New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC(1A))
- Old regime available via Form 10-IEA under Section 115BAC(6)

RELEVANT SECTIONS:
- Section 44AD – Presumptive taxation for eligible businesses
- Section 44ADA – Presumptive taxation for specified professions
- Section 44AE – Presumptive taxation for goods transport
- Section 139(1) – Return filing
- Section 115BAC(1A) – Default new tax regime
- Section 115BAC(6) – Opt out of new regime
- 7th Proviso to Section 139(1) – Mandatory filing conditions

==============================================================================
ITR-5
==============================================================================

APPLICABLE TO:
- Firms (including partnership firms)
- Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs)
- Association of Persons (AOP)
- Body of Individuals (BOI)
- Artificial Juridical Person (AJP)
- Cooperative Societies
- Local Authorities
- Business Trusts (not covered under Section 139(4A) to 139(4F))
- Investment Funds

WHO CANNOT USE ITR-5:
- Individual taxpayers (use ITR-1, ITR-2, ITR-3, or ITR-4)
- Hindu Undivided Families / HUF (use ITR-2 or ITR-3)
- Companies (use ITR-6)
- Entities filing under Section 139(4A) – Charitable/Religious Trusts (use ITR-7)
- Entities filing under Section 139(4B) – Political Parties (use ITR-7)
- Entities filing under Section 139(4C) – Research institutions, news agencies (use ITR-7)
- Entities filing under Section 139(4D) – Universities and Colleges (use ITR-7)
- Entities filing under Section 139(4E) – Business Trusts filing under ITR-7
- Entities filing under Section 139(4F) – Investment Funds filing under ITR-7

ALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- Business or Profession income (Section 28)
- Capital Gains (Section 45)
- Income from house property (Section 22)
- Income from other sources (Section 56)
- Foreign income (for firms/LLPs with foreign operations)
- Partner's remuneration and interest from firm

NO INCOME LIMIT for ITR-5.

RELEVANT SECTIONS:
- Section 2(23) – Definition of Firm
- Section 28 – Business income
- Section 40(b) – Allowable remuneration/interest to partners
- Section 45 – Capital gains
- Section 139(1) – Return filing
- Section 115JC – Alternate Minimum Tax on AOP/BOI
- Section 115BAC – Tax regime (firms and LLPs)

==============================================================================
ITR-6
==============================================================================

APPLICABLE TO:
- ALL Companies (Domestic and Foreign)
- Companies claiming deductions EXCEPT those claiming exemption under Section 11
  (which applies to charitable/religious trusts - those use ITR-7)

WHO CANNOT USE ITR-6:
- Companies claiming exemption under Section 11 (Income from property held for
  charitable or religious purposes) - they must use ITR-7

ALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- Business or Profession income / Profits and Gains (PGBP - Section 28)
- Capital Gains (Section 45)
- Income from house property (Section 22)
- Income from other sources (Section 56)
- Foreign income
- Special rate incomes (e.g. LTCG under Section 112A)

NO INCOME LIMIT for ITR-6.

SPECIAL CASES:
- Domestic companies pay tax at 22% (Section 115BAA) or 15% for new
  manufacturing companies (Section 115BAB)
- Foreign companies pay tax at 40% on Indian income
- Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT) under Section 115JB applies
- Companies claiming exemption under Sections 10AA (SEZ units) must
  file before due date

RELEVANT SECTIONS:
- Section 11 – Exemption for charitable trusts (NOT applicable to ITR-6 filers)
- Section 28 – Business income (PGBP)
- Section 45 – Capital gains
- Section 115BA – Tax rate for certain domestic companies
- Section 115BAA – Reduced rate (22%) for domestic companies (no exemptions/deductions)
- Section 115BAB – 15% rate for new manufacturing companies
- Section 115JB – Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT) at 15% of book profit
- Section 139(1) – Return filing
- Section 80-IA, 80-IB, 80-IC – Profit-linked deductions for companies

==============================================================================
ITR-7
==============================================================================

APPLICABLE TO:
Entities REQUIRED to file return under specific sections:

1. Section 139(4A) – Charitable or Religious Trusts and Institutions
   (income derived from property held for charitable/religious purposes)

2. Section 139(4B) – Political Parties
   (if total income exceeds the exemption limit before claiming deductions)

3. Section 139(4C) – Specific Institutions such as:
   - Scientific research associations (Section 10(21))
   - News agencies (Section 10(22B))
   - Trade unions, associations (Section 10(24))
   - Funds and institutions (Section 10(23C))
   - Universities and educational institutions (Section 10(23C)(iiiad)/(vi))

4. Section 139(4D) – Universities, Colleges, and other institutions
   (required to furnish returns even if not liable to pay tax)

5. Section 139(4E) – Business Trusts (Real Estate Investment Trusts / REITs,
   Infrastructure Investment Trusts / InvITs)

6. Section 139(4F) – Investment Funds (Category I and II AIFs registered
   with SEBI under the SEBI Regulations)

WHO CANNOT USE ITR-7:
- Individual taxpayers (use ITR-1 to ITR-3)
- HUF (use ITR-2 or ITR-3)
- Companies NOT claiming exemption under Section 11 (use ITR-6)
- Firms, LLPs, AOP, BOI (use ITR-5)
- Regular trusts not required under Section 139(4A) to 139(4F)

ALLOWED INCOME SOURCES:
- Depends on entity type - can include all income heads
- Charitable trusts: income from property + voluntary contributions
- Political parties: all income sources
- Business trusts/investment funds: pass-through income, business income

TAX EXEMPTIONS AVAILABLE:
- Section 11 – Exemption for income applied to charitable/religious purposes
- Section 12 – Exemption for voluntary contributions to trusts
- Section 12A/12AB – Registration requirement for trust exemptions
- Section 13 – Cases where exemptions are NOT available (forfeiture conditions)

RELEVANT SECTIONS:
- Section 11 – Income from property held for charitable/religious purposes
- Section 12 – Income of trusts and institutions
- Section 12A / 12AB – Registration for trusts (mandatory for exemption)
- Section 13 – Denial of exemption under Sections 11 and 12
- Section 80G – Deduction to donors (trust must be registered)
- Section 139(4A) – Charitable/Religious trusts return filing
- Section 139(4B) – Political parties return filing
- Section 139(4C) – Specified institutions return filing
- Section 139(4D) – Universities/Colleges return filing
- Section 139(4E) – Business trusts return filing
- Section 139(4F) – Investment funds return filing
- Section 115BBC – Anonymous donations taxable at 30%

==============================================================================
QUICK COMPARISON: WHICH ITR FORM TO USE
==============================================================================

Individual with salary only, total income ≤ Rs 50L, resident → ITR-1
Individual with multiple properties / capital gains, no business → ITR-2
Individual / HUF with business or profession income → ITR-3
Individual / HUF / firm with presumptive income, ≤ Rs 50L, resident → ITR-4
Firm / LLP / AOP / BOI / Co-op society → ITR-5
Company (not exempt under Section 11) → ITR-6
Charitable trust / political party / research institution → ITR-7

NRI with salary + capital gains → ITR-2
NRI with business income → ITR-3

KEY INCOME LIMITS:
- ITR-1: Total income ≤ Rs. 50 lakh; Agricultural income ≤ Rs. 5,000
- ITR-4: Total income ≤ Rs. 50 lakh
- ITR-2, ITR-3, ITR-5, ITR-6, ITR-7: No income limit

PRESUMPTIVE TAXATION LIMITS (AY 2025-26):
- Section 44AD business: Turnover ≤ Rs. 2 crore (Rs. 3 crore if 95%+ digital)
- Section 44ADA profession: Receipts ≤ Rs. 75 lakh (Rs. 150 lakh if 95%+ digital)
- Section 44AE transport: Up to 10 goods vehicles
